FAQ

Get answers to common questions about Nebula AI

What is Nebula AI?
Nebula AI is a decentralized GPU marketplace where anyone can rent compute power, stake $NAI for rewards, and access AI tools—all without relying on centralized cloud providers.
How does renting a GPU work?
Users can browse available GPU resources on our marketplace, select the specifications they need, and rent them for their AI workloads. Payment is made in $NAI tokens, and the rented GPUs can be accessed immediately through our platform.
What is staking, and why should I care?
Staking allows you to lock up your $NAI tokens to help secure the network and earn rewards. By staking, you contribute to the ecosystem's stability while earning passive income from transaction fees and network rewards.
How is Nebula AI different from AWS, Google Cloud, or Vast AI?
Unlike centralized cloud providers, Nebula AI is fully decentralized, meaning no single entity controls the network. This results in lower costs, better privacy, and more equitable rewards for both GPU providers and users. Our platform also integrates native AI tools and a token economy that rewards participation.
What is the Super-App Marketplace?
The Super-App Marketplace is our integrated platform where users can access various AI tools and services that run on our decentralized GPU network. This includes pre-trained models, development tools, and specialized AI applications - all accessible through a single interface.
Is Nebula AI secure?
Yes, Nebula AI employs multiple layers of security including blockchain-based verification, encrypted communications, and decentralized storage. All transactions are transparently recorded on-chain, and our smart contracts have undergone extensive security audits.
